What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. The AI tools you will encounter most often are called Large Language Models (LLMs). Think of them as incredibly well-read conversation partners who have absorbed vast amounts of text from books, websites, and documents.

How Does It Work? (The Simple Version)

Imagine you started a sentence with "The cat sat on the..." and asked a thousand people to finish it. Most would say "mat" or "couch" or "bed." AI works similarly. It has learned patterns from billions of examples of human writing and predicts what words should come next based on what you have typed.

This means AI is excellent at understanding context, following instructions, generating ideas, explaining concepts, and helping you think through problems. However, it is not looking things up in a database or searching the internet in real-time (unless it has specific tools to do so). It is pattern-matching based on its training.

What AI Can and Cannot Do

AI is Great At

  • Brainstorming ideas
  • Explaining concepts in different ways
  • Drafting and editing text
  • Summarizing long documents
  • Answering questions
  • Helping you think through decisions
  • Creating outlines and plans
  • Acting as a thought partner

AI Has Limitations

  • Can be confidently wrong ("hallucinations")
  • No real-time information unless connected to web
  • Cannot remember previous conversations
  • Works best with clear context and instructions

meta.ai

Created by Meta (Facebook). Free to use with strong image generation capabilities. Integrated into Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp.

Best Practices: How to Work with AI

Before diving into specific prompt techniques, here are foundational principles that will make every interaction more productive:

Have a Conversation, Not a Transaction

AI works best when you engage in back-and-forth dialogue. Your first prompt does not need to be perfect. Start somewhere, see what you get, and refine from there. Ask follow-up questions. Say "that is not quite what I meant" or "can you focus more on X?" The AI will adjust.

Always Verify Important Information

AI can be confidently wrong. For anything consequential - facts, figures, legal information, medical advice - verify with authoritative sources. Think of AI as a helpful first draft or starting point, not the final word.

Start New Chats for New Topics

Each conversation builds context. If you have been discussing recipes and suddenly want help with a work email, the AI might still be thinking about food. Starting fresh gives you a clean slate and often better results.

Provide Context Generously

The AI only knows what you tell it. Sharing relevant background - who you are, what you are trying to accomplish, who your audience is, what constraints you have - dramatically improves the quality of responses.

Be Specific About What You Want

"Help me write an email" will get you something generic. "Help me write a friendly but professional email to my landlord requesting a repair, keeping it under 150 words" will get you something much more useful.

You Cannot Get It Wrong

There is no "correct" way to use AI. Experiment freely. Try weird things. Ask silly questions. The worst that happens is you get a response that is not helpful, and you try again. This is a space for curiosity and play.

Prompt Techniques: From Basic to Brilliant

The following sections introduce 18 prompting techniques, organized from simplest to most sophisticated. We will explore these through two scenarios to contextualize the prompting methods:

Scenario A: Planning a Community Event You are organizing a summer festival for your neighborhood.
Scenario B: Starting a Small Business You are launching a small bakery in your town.

As you read through these examples, notice how each technique builds on previous ones. Feel free to mix and match - the best prompts often combine multiple techniques. Click any card to expand it.

Foundational Prompts

These are your everyday workhorses - simple, direct, and effective for most tasks.

What it is: A straightforward question seeking information or an explanation. No frills, just asking what you want to know.
Example Prompt
What permits do I need to hold an outdoor festival in New York State?
Pro Tip

Start here when you are unsure. A simple question often gets you 80% of what you need.

What it is: Asking AI to explain a concept, often at a specific level or for a specific audience. Great for learning.
Example Prompt
Explain food safety requirements for selling baked goods in New York as if I have never run a food business before.
Pro Tip

Specify your knowledge level. "Explain like I am five" or "assume I have a background in X" shapes the response significantly.

What it is: Asking AI to generate a list of ideas, options, or items. Excellent for brainstorming and ensuring you have not missed anything.
Example Prompt
Give me a list of 15 potential activities for a family-friendly summer festival.
Pro Tip

Ask for more items than you need. You can always trim, but it is harder to expand a short list.

What it is: Asking AI to compare options, weigh pros and cons, or analyze trade-offs. Helpful when facing decisions.
Example Prompt
Compare renting a commercial kitchen versus building a home kitchen for my bakery business. Consider cost, regulations, and flexibility.
Pro Tip

Give AI your actual constraints. "I have $5,000 to start" changes the analysis dramatically.

What it is: Using AI as a thought partner to generate creative ideas. Unlike list generation, this is more exploratory and open-ended.
Example Prompt
I am planning a summer festival and want it to feel different from typical community events. Brainstorm some creative themes or unique angles we could take.
Pro Tip

Follow up on ideas you like. Say "tell me more about idea #3" or "how could we combine ideas 2 and 5?"

What it is: Asking AI to create a reusable template, framework, or structure that you can fill in yourself.
Example Prompt
Create a template for a vendor application form for my festival. Include all the fields I should collect from food vendors.
Pro Tip

Ask for templates in formats you can actually use. Specify if you need it as a Word document outline, spreadsheet structure, or simple text.

Context-Setting Prompts

These techniques improve AI responses by providing background information or setting expectations upfront.

What it is: Asking AI to adopt a specific persona or expertise. This shapes the perspective and depth of responses.
Example Prompt
You are an experienced event planner who has organized over 50 community festivals. Help me create a realistic timeline for planning a summer festival that is six months away.
Pro Tip

The role you assign affects tone, vocabulary, and what details AI emphasizes. A "marketing expert" gives different advice than a "community organizer."

What it is: Front-loading your prompt with relevant background information so AI understands your specific situation.
Example Prompt
Context: I am opening a small bakery in a town of 8,000 people. There is one existing bakery that focuses on bread. I want to specialize in custom cakes and pastries. My budget is $20,000 for equipment. I have baking experience but no business experience. Question: What should my first three months look like?
Pro Tip

More context almost always helps. Include your constraints, goals, audience, and any relevant history.

What it is: Explicitly stating who the output is for, which shapes tone, complexity, and content choices.
Example Prompt
Write a one-page description of our summer festival for the local newspaper. The audience is families in our community who might not have heard of the event.
Pro Tip

Consider multiple audiences. You might need one version for sponsors, another for volunteers, another for attendees.

Structured Prompts

These techniques add structure to your requests, helping AI organize its thinking and output.

What it is: Asking AI to break down a process into sequential steps. Great for complex procedures or learning new skills.
Example Prompt
Walk me through the step-by-step process of getting a food handler's license in New York, from application to certification.
Pro Tip

Specify how detailed you want the steps. "High-level overview" versus "detailed enough that someone could follow without other resources."

What it is: Adding specific limitations or requirements that the response must meet. Forces more practical, usable outputs.
Example Prompt
Create a marketing plan for my bakery grand opening. Constraints: budget is under $500, I have two weeks to prepare, and I can only work on evenings and weekends.
Pro Tip

Real constraints lead to real solutions. Do not hold back on sharing your actual limitations.

What it is: Explicitly requesting how you want the output structured - as a table, bullet points, numbered list, paragraph, etc.
Example Prompt
Create a festival planning checklist organized by timeframe: 6 months out, 3 months out, 1 month out, 1 week out, and day-of. Format as a table with task, owner, and status columns.
Pro Tip

Tables work great for comparing options. Bullet points for quick reference. Paragraphs for things you will share with others.

Learning by Example

These techniques use examples to show AI exactly what you want, rather than just describing it.

What it is: Providing examples of what you want before asking AI to generate more. AI learns the pattern from your examples and continues it.
Example Prompt
I need taglines for my bakery. Here are two I like: "Where every crumb tells a story" and "Baked with love, served with joy." Generate 10 more taglines in this same warm, personal style.
Pro Tip

Two or three examples is usually enough. Make sure your examples clearly show the pattern you want.

What it is: Providing a sample of writing and asking AI to match its tone, voice, or style for new content.
Example Prompt
Here is our festival's previous announcement: [paste example]. Write this year's announcement in the same friendly, community-focused voice, but for our new western theme.
Pro Tip

This works for matching your own writing style, your organization's voice, or even mimicking a format you have seen elsewhere.

Iterative Refinement

These techniques embrace the conversational nature of AI, building better outputs through back-and-forth dialogue.

What it is: Asking AI to review and critique something you have created, identifying weaknesses and suggesting improvements.
Example Prompt
Here is my draft budget for the festival. Review it critically - what am I likely underestimating? What costs am I probably missing? Where should I build in contingency?
Pro Tip

Ask for specific types of feedback. "Critique for clarity" differs from "critique for persuasiveness" or "find logical gaps."

What it is: Starting with a basic output and progressively improving it through targeted follow-up requests.
Example Prompt
Start: "Write a description of my bakery." Then: "Make it warmer and more personal." Then: "Add a sentence about our commitment to local ingredients." Then: "Shorten it to 50 words."
Pro Tip

Small, specific refinements work better than asking for everything at once. It is like sculpting - start with the rough shape, then refine details.

Advanced Techniques

These sophisticated techniques unlock more powerful uses of AI for complex thinking and planning.

What it is: Asking AI to show its reasoning process, thinking through a problem step-by-step before reaching a conclusion. Leads to more accurate and nuanced answers.
Example Prompt
I am trying to decide whether to open my bakery downtown (higher rent, more foot traffic) or in a neighborhood location (lower rent, need to build customer base). Think through this decision step by step, considering all relevant factors before making a recommendation.
Pro Tip

This technique is especially valuable for complex decisions. The reasoning process often reveals factors you had not considered.

What it is: Asking AI to help you write better prompts. It is prompting about prompting - using AI to improve how you communicate with AI.
Example Prompt
I want to get AI help planning my festival, but I am not sure how to ask effectively. What information should I include in my prompt to get the most useful planning help? What questions should I be asking?
Pro Tip

When stuck, ask AI how to ask. It can help you identify what context is missing or what questions you should be exploring.

Putting It All Together

The best prompts often combine multiple techniques. This example weaves together role assignment, context dump, constraint specification, format request, and step-by-step instruction:

Combined Example Prompt
You are an experienced small business advisor who specializes in food businesses. Context: I am opening a bakery in a small town (population 8,000) with a $20,000 budget. I have strong baking skills but no business experience. My goal is to be profitable within 12 months. Constraints: I can only work on setup part-time for the next 3 months while I finish my current job. Task: Create a 12-month launch plan. Format it as a timeline with monthly milestones, key tasks, and estimated costs. Think through the logical sequence - what needs to happen before what?
Pro Tip

Notice how this prompt gives AI everything it needs: expertise to draw on, specific context, clear constraints, defined format, and explicit instruction to reason through the sequence.
