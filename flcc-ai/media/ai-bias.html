AI Bias

October 2025 Dave Ghidiu ~60 minutes

About This Webinar

AI systems don't come from nowhere — they're built by people, trained on human-generated data, and deployed into complex social contexts. That means the biases present in our world have a way of sneaking into our AI tools, sometimes in obvious ways and sometimes in deeply subtle ones.

In this session, we unpacked the five dimensions of AI bias — from how training data is collected to how algorithms weight certain outcomes — and looked at concrete case studies where bias showed up in high-stakes applications like hiring, healthcare, and criminal justice.

More importantly, we didn't stop at "here's the problem." Attendees left with a practical framework for identifying bias risks in the tools they use and a set of mitigation strategies they could start applying immediately — whether they're educators, administrators, or business leaders.

What We Covered

The five dimensions of AI bias: Historical bias, representation bias, measurement bias, aggregation bias, and evaluation bias — each with real-world examples drawn from education, business, and government.

Why "neutral" data isn't neutral: A look at how seemingly objective datasets carry embedded assumptions, and how those assumptions get amplified at scale.

Mitigation strategies: Practical techniques for auditing AI tools you're already using, questions to ask vendors, and how to build bias awareness into your team's AI decision-making process.

Live demonstration: We ran a live demo comparing outputs from popular AI tools on identical prompts to surface where bias shows up in practice.

Who It's For

This webinar is designed for educators, administrators, HR professionals, and organizational leaders — anyone who is using or evaluating AI tools and wants to use them more responsibly. No technical background required.
