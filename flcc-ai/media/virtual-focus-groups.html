Virtual Focus Groups

November 2025 Dave Ghidiu & Debora Ortloff ~75 minutes

About This Webinar

Focus groups have always been a powerful tool for gathering qualitative insights — but they're also time-consuming, expensive to facilitate, and challenging to analyze at scale. AI is changing that equation, and not in a way that replaces human judgment, but in a way that amplifies it.

In this session, Dave Ghidiu and Debora Ortloff walked through how AI tools are being used at every stage of the virtual focus group process — from designing effective discussion guides to facilitating sessions more smoothly to synthesizing hours of qualitative data into actionable themes.

This wasn't a theoretical overview. The session was grounded in real examples from projects the FLX AI Hub team has worked on, and attendees had the chance to ask questions and explore how these tools might apply to their own research and organizational needs.

What We Covered

Designing better focus groups with AI: How to use AI to stress-test your discussion guide, identify potential blind spots, and develop probing questions that surface deeper insights.

AI-assisted facilitation: Tools and techniques for real-time support during sessions — including live transcription, sentiment tracking, and flags for emerging themes.

Synthesis at scale: How to use AI tools to analyze transcripts, identify patterns across multiple sessions, and generate structured reports — cutting synthesis time from days to hours.

Ethical considerations: Participant consent, data privacy, and ensuring AI analysis doesn't flatten the nuance that makes qualitative research valuable in the first place.

Live walkthrough: A step-by-step demonstration using a sample transcript processed through multiple AI tools, showing exactly how to prompt for useful qualitative analysis.

Who It's For

Researchers, program evaluators, HR teams, marketing professionals, and anyone who conducts qualitative research or community listening sessions. Especially valuable for organizations that need to move quickly on insights but still care about doing it right.
