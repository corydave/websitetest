Please Don't Tell Me to Use AI

March 2026 Dave Ghidiu

AI can do a lot. It can draft, summarize, brainstorm, and research. It can pull together information from a thousand sources in seconds, and it keeps getting better.

But there is one thing it genuinely cannot do - it cannot tell us what any one person knows. Not the stuff that matters most.


AI is trained on what has been written. People are shaped by what has been lived - those are not the same thing.

A firefighter sizing up a building hears something in the creak of a floor. She reads the color of smoke curling under a door. She knows - from years of walking into structures that wanted to hurt her - which way to move and which hallway to never enter.

An ER nurse listens to a patient say they feel "fine" and knows, without being able to name exactly why, that something is off. The word choice, the pause, the way a hand moved. That instinct is thousands of patient interactions compressed into what looks like a hunch. No model trained on medical literature has seen what he has seen.

A summer camp counselor with fifteen years of experience knows, within the first hour of a new session, which kids are going to struggle that night. Not from a checklist - from something in how they hold their shoulders while unpacking. That read is not in any training manual. It lives in her, built from thousands of small moments she probably stopped consciously noticing years ago.

The reference librarian who has been at the same desk for twenty years can hear the question behind the question. The line cook who knows a sauce is right by the sound of the simmer. The school bus driver who knows which kid is having a bad morning before the kid has said a word.

This is knowledge that is real. Knowledge that is unwritten.

Don't underestimate the power of you. You are the synthesis of a specific vantage point, a particular set of years, an exact combination of wins and hard lessons. Nobody else has that. Not a single person has looked at any given corner of the world through the same set of lenses, in the same sequence, with the same stakes. AI can surface the consensus. Only you can offer your expertise. The lessons you learned from specific failures. There is something worth celebrating in the fact that every person carries knowledge that exists nowhere else.

So know that when I ask you something, I'm not looking for advice on using AI. I'm asking you because I know that AI cannot know what you know. I'm asking you for your opinion. Your expertise. Your unique insight.

I recognize that your particular experience has value that no prompt can replicate. Don't tell me to use AI. I am using AI. I'm prompting your Acquired Intuition.
